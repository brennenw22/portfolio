AI Model Performance Benchmarking Harness

Summary: The article presents the design and implementation of a generalized benchmark harness to evaluate the performance of artificial intelligence (AI) models in a reproducible and scalable way prior to deployment. AI models—especially deep learning models—are often optimized to run efficiently on resource-constrained devices, but these optimizations can affect accuracy. The proposed Performance Benchmark Harness (PBH) provides a unified inference loop, tracks key metrics such as throughput, latency, memory usage, and accuracy, and generates detailed performance profiles to compare models before and after optimization.

  • Key idea: Comprehensive benchmarking across multiple metrics.
  • Impact: Improves reliability and deployment decisions.
  • Takeaway: Better evaluation leads to better AI performance in real-world use.

05

AI Agents in Construction and Business Systems

Summary: These articles explain how AI agents are transforming construction and broader business operations by automating tasks, improving decision-making, and increasing efficiency. Unlike basic chatbots, AI agents can act autonomously, execute workflows, and interact with real-time data. In construction, they help manage workflows, track costs, automate RFIs and reporting, and identify risks using project data. Tools like Domo’s AI Agent Builder and MCP Server take this further by connecting enterprise data directly to AI systems, allowing organizations to build customized agents that analyze data, generate reports, and execute business processes. Overall, AI agents are shifting industries toward more integrated, data-driven operations where technology enhances human productivity rather than replacing it.

  • Key idea: AI agents move beyond chatbots by executing tasks and workflows using real-time data.
  • Impact: Improves efficiency, reduces errors, and enables faster decision-making in construction and business.
  • Takeaway: The future of AI lies in fully integrated systems where AI actively supports operations.
